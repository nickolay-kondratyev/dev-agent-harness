## What Changed

### Before (3 concurrent actors)
1. **Executor health loop** — reads `isQAPending`, suppresses health checks
2. **HTTP server** — receives `/signal/user-question`, sets `isQAPending = true`, forwards to coordinator
3. **Q&A coordinator coroutine** — dedicated per-session coroutine, owns `QAPendingState`, collects answers, delivers via TMUX

### After (2 concurrent actors)
1. **HTTP server** — receives `/signal/user-question`, appends to `SessionEntry.questionQueue` (thread-safe)
2. **Executor's health-aware await loop** — checks `questionQueue` on each tick; when non-empty, drains queue, collects answers via `UserQuestionHandler`, batch-delivers via `AckedPayloadSender`, resumes signal-await

### Key simplifications
- `isQAPending` is now a derived property (`questionQueue.isNotEmpty()`) — no separate boolean to keep in sync
- No dedicated coordinator coroutine lifecycle (startup, shutdown, orphan risk)
- Ping suppression trivially correct — executor owns both health checks and Q&A
- Fewer concurrency boundaries = fewer race conditions
