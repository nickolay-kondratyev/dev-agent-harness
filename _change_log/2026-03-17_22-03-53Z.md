## What changed

Updated `doc/core/UserQuestionHandler.md` to explicitly document that `StdinUserQuestionHandler` intentionally has no timeout.

## Why

Ticket nid_zcx2kqker77ku9xvwsq607tt6_E raised the question of adding a configurable timeout. The decision was: **no timeout**. The harness is for semi-attended workflows — the human operator may be in a meeting or on a walk, and failing the workflow on inactivity would be worse than pausing it.

## Spec changes
- Updated Timeout row: "None — intentionally blocks indefinitely."
- Updated "If human is away" row: "Blocks indefinitely. The workflow pauses and resumes when the human returns."
- Added "Design Decision: No Timeout on Stdin Q&A" subsection with full rationale and consequence warning for unattended environments.
